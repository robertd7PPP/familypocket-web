Days Since

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 26 June 2026

Days Since is a simple tracking app. Your data never leaves your own devices — there is no Days Since server, no account, and no analytics.

What we collect

Nothing. Days Since collects no personal information and sends no data to us or any third party.

Where your data is stored

Everything you add — events, resets, and reminders — is stored privately on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. If you are signed into iCloud, Apple automatically syncs this data across your own devices through iCloud's private CloudKit database. This sync is end-to-end encrypted and is governed by Apple's iCloud Privacy Policy.

Notifications

If you enable reminders, Days Since schedules local notifications on your device. These are processed entirely on-device and no notification content is transmitted to us.

Purchases

The optional "Days Since Pro" upgrade is processed entirely through Apple's App Store. We never see your payment details. Purchase records are managed by Apple and tied to your Apple ID. You can restore a purchase at any time from within the app.

Calendar access

If you choose to import events from your Apple Calendar, the app requests read-only access to your calendars. Calendar data is read once to populate your events and is never stored externally or shared.

Third-party services

Days Since does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, crash-reporting, or tracking SDKs.

Children

Days Since does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes

If we ever change this policy in a material way, we will update the date above and note the change in the App Store release notes.
